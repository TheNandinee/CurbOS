01 · The problem

A blocked lane is a parked vehicle nobody dispatched a crew to.

Parking-induced congestion is just stopped vehicles holding live lanes — delivery riders, cars outside restaurants, autos waiting for fares. BTP can see the jam, but not which 150 metres is blocked, for how long, by whom. Without that, scarce crews patrol by gut.

02 · Why it's still unsolved

BTP has the cameras. It's missing the metric.

The tools that exist measure flow and length, not the stopped-vehicle cause — and none of them tell a crew where to go next.

ASTraM

Length, not cause

BTP's award-winning platform shows congestion length in metres and hotspots. It can't say a jam is caused by stopped vehicles, or which class.

BATCS

Signals, not curbs

Adaptive signals optimise green time at junctions. They do nothing about a lane blocked mid-link by a parked LCV.

VIOLATION CV

Fines, not flow

Helmet/seatbelt detection punishes after the fact. It never tells you which obstruction to clear to move traffic now.

03 · The insight

Optimise the scarce thing: the crew. Fix the controllable thing: the fleet.

Most obstruction is cars and autos — BTP can only clear those. Delivery vehicles are different: a real and growing slice, and the one class software can actually redirect. So CurbOS clears everything smarter, and stops the controllable part before it starts.

MEASURE

Obstruction-minutes

The missing primitive: how long each segment is blocked, by which vehicle class — from cameras BTP already runs.

OPTIMISE

Vehicle-minutes per crew-hour

Send the next crew to the segment that clears the most blocking per trip — not the spot they always patrol.

CLOSE THE LOOP

Fleet advisory

Push "enforcement active here" into rider apps so delivery vehicles stop blocking it at all.

04 · How it works

A loop that plugs into ASTraM — no new hardware.

Camera → obstruction engine → dispatch optimiser + fleet API. Every step uses infrastructure BTP already owns and a workflow they already run (they tow near malls today).

01

Detect

Stopped-vehicle detection on existing CCTV measures obstruction-minutes per segment, by class.

02

Rank

Segments ranked by live blocking, not chronic reputation — the new layer for ASTraM.

03

Dispatch

The optimiser tells each crew where to go next to clear the most vehicle-minutes per hour.

04

Comply

Fleets get a real-time advisory and reroute their stops — obstruction prevented at source.
